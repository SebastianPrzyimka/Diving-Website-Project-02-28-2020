Advanced Diver

Why is it worth it? The advanced PADI ADVANCED OPEN WATER DIVER (PADI AOWD) course will allow you to increase your skills underwater. After completing five dives: deep - below 20 meters deep, navigational and three selected specialized dives (including night dives), you will learn about the underwater environment and learn about various types of dives that may become your passion.

What do you need to try? Minimum age: 15 years (you can complete the Junior PADI ADVANCED OPEN WATER DIVER course (PADI AOWD junior) at the age of 12-15, however the maximum diving depth is 21 m), PADI Open Water Diver Certificate (PADI OWD) or a certificate of another organization allowing diving without an instructor.

What will you do? During the PADI ADVANCED OPEN WATER DIVER course program (PADI AOWD) five dives are required, including navigational dives, deep dives and three selected from the list below: diving above 300 m above sea level (Altitude Diver) Boat Diver Diving (Drift Diver) diving in a dry suit (Dry Suit Diver) Multilevel Diver Night Diver buoyancy diving (Peak Performance Buoyancy) Search and Recovery Diver knowledge of underwater life (Underwater Naturalist) underwater filming (Underwater Videographer) Course PADI AOWD Underwater Photographer wreck diving (Wreck Diver) Recommended number of hours: 15 hours, minimum two diving days.

What do you need? Willingness to try an underwater adventure, Training materials - you will receive in the Diving Center, Logbook (dive book / dive record), two photographs.

What will you get after this course? It is possible to dive to a depth of 30 m with a diver of the same degree. If you are at least 15 years old, you can start: the PADI Wreck Diver wreck course or the PADI Deep Diver (up to 40 m deep), the PADI Ice Diver course and the PDI Cave DIver cave diving course. Everyone can start the PADI Rescue Diver or PADI Junior Rescue Diver course.
