Diver Master

What can PADI Divemaster do after completing the course? Work at the Diving Center around the world as a dive organizer and dive guide, help the diving instructor during training for lower diving levels, i.e. PADI Open Water Diver (PADI OWD), PADI Advanced Open Water Diver (PADI AOWD), PADI Rescue Diver, independently conduct diving groups from the PADI Scuba Diver degree, train and grant PADI Skin Diver diving certificates, conduct the PADI Discover Snorkeling training program (breathless diving exercises), conduct PADI Scuba Review diving course exams for already certified divers, lead groups of certified divers in the Discover Local Diving program, teach PADI EFF procedures to all divers who have already been certified by the Instructor.

What do you need to try? Minimum age: 18 years old, PADI Advanced Open Water Diver Certificate (PADI AOWD) or another organization's certificate qualifying for this course , PADI Rescue Diver certificate or another organization's certificate qualifying for this course , Doctor's decision about no contraindications to diving, A minimum of 20 confirmed dives,

What would you do? During the PADI Divemaster course you will learn how to lead a group of divers both in theory and in practice in the pool and in open waters. You will take part in the PADI Open Water Diver (PADI OWD) and PADI Discover Scuba Diver (PADI DSD) courses to acquire practice in assisting the Instructor during training with beginner divers in the pool and open waters. You will learn how to identify the most common problems in novice divers underwater and how to help young dive students to make their dives become equally safe. You will gain advanced theoretical knowledge and learn PADI standards that are used by all professional PADI divers in diving centers around the world to increase the level of service for less experienced divers. You will learn how to be a role model for beginner divers.

How long does this course take? The course can last as long as you get the right qualifications, but not longer than 12 months.

What do you need? Training materials - a recommended set of training materials for Divemaster, Logbook (dive book / dive record), You must have at least 60 dives logged in to complete the PADI DIVEMASTER course. two photographs. What you will get after that

What will you get after this course? If you have completed the PADI DIVEMASTER course, you can take the PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor (PADI OWSI) course or the PADI Assistant Instructor course. You will receive a special PADI DIVEMASTER number that will entitle you to log in to the PADI PRO zone on the PADI website.
